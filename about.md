# Kenneth Brandt

I work at the intersection of **GCP/GxP quality systems, quality operations, and operational governance**, with 20+ years of experience across biopharma, sterile manufacturing, biologics/CDMOs, consulting, and information systems.

I help teams build **inspection-ready systems that work day to day** across clinical and manufacturing environments, with evidence that holds up under FDA, EMA, and MHRA scrutiny.

My perspective is shaped by more than regulated quality alone. In addition to GxP work, my background includes information systems, MBA-level business training that included design thinking, and military service. That combination influences how I think about organizations: not just as policies and org charts, but as systems that must remain clear, resilient, and executable under pressure.

## What this site is about

I write about how organizations build systems that people can actually run.

Sometimes that means GxP quality systems, inspection readiness, and vendor oversight. Sometimes it means cybersecurity, AI governance, data quality, or agile operating models. In every case, the underlying question is similar: does the system translate intent into clear action, real accountability, and dependable execution when the stakes are high?

This site explores that question across regulated operations, technology, governance, and leadership.

## Who this site is for

- Biotech and biologics teams building or upgrading GxP systems
- Clinical and manufacturing leaders who need inspection readiness without overbuilding
- Teams adopting new tools, including AI-enabled workflows, that need governance, monitoring, and traceability
- Operators, managers, and leaders who care about systems that work in practice
